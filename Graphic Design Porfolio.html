Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

When creating a design, I make sure that it is my own work or that I have given credit to the original creator. Plagiarism in graphic design cannot be tolerated. Furthermore, each designer has something different to contribute. Copying others work will not inspire novel ideas. Furthermore, utilizing different applications when creating a design will help enhance its contrasting elements. Using Canva, for example, before transitioning over to an Adobe application will allow a designer to have a larger toolbox to work with.

2. Leverage Design Principles.


Within each of my designs, I have consciously made decisions to incorporate design principles we have been discussing throughout the semester. These principles include contrast in color, font choices, empty or white space, and symbolism.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

Throughout the course of this semester, I have and continue to discuss weekly design projects with my peers. Each of us take a few minutes to share our designs and describe our successes as well as what we struggled with during the creative process. Furthermore, we each give each other feedback, whether it’s through answering questions the designer had or asking clarifying questions of our own.



4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

An additional concept that I have been learning this semester is the process of revising work after completing a first draft. Design is quite similar to writing in this way, as written works also need to be reformed prior to a final submission. Taking time away from a design and coming back to it with a fresh perspective can help you think of possible edits or alterations that can be made to a project.

5. Learn to present your work.


As of our semester’s midterm, we have been provided with three opportunities to share our work with others. Whether this took place in front of the entire class or in small groups, I have been able to access the projector multiple times to showcase my design projects. Not only does this help with gaining confidence to present work in front of an audience, it also has allowed me to gain an understanding of what an audience needs to follow along with a presentation.
